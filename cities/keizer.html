Keizer Accident Corridors

Where Keizer Accidents Happen Most

Keizer sits between Salem and the rural Marion County corridor, with I-5 running directly through the city. This creates a unique traffic pattern — heavy agricultural transport mixed with suburban commuter traffic on roads not designed for current volumes.

🛣️
I-5 Keizer / Chemawa Exit Ramps

The Chemawa Rd and Chemawa exit ramps off I-5 north of Salem are significant accident locations where highway-speed traffic meets stop-and-go suburban congestion. Semi-truck traffic from the I-5 agricultural corridor adds serious weight and force to crashes at these interchanges.

🌾
River Rd N — Keizer's Main Corridor

River Rd N is Keizer's primary commercial arterial and runs directly past our clinic. The combination of residential driveways, commercial pull-outs, and steady commuter traffic creates consistent T-bone and rear-end crash conditions — particularly at the River Rd / Chemawa Rd intersection which is among Marion County's busiest.

🚜
Chemawa Rd NE — Farm-to-Market Hazards

Chemawa Rd connects Keizer to surrounding agricultural areas. Slow-moving farm equipment, seasonal crop transport trucks, and commuters accelerating through rural intersections create crash conditions that are distinct from purely urban accidents. Side-impact crashes at Chemawa's rural intersections are particularly common in harvest season (August–October).

🏘️
Lockhaven Dr — Residential Collector

Lockhaven Dr funnels significant residential traffic into Keizer's commercial core. Speeding through residential zones, school zone violations, and limited sight lines at intersections make this one of Keizer's higher-injury residential corridors. Pedestrian and cyclist involvement is increasing as the area develops.
