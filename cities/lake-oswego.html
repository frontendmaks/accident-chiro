Where Lake Oswego Accidents Happen Most

🛣️
I-5 South — Lake Oswego Exit Ramps

The Kruse Way and Boones Ferry Road exits off I-5 near Lake Oswego are among the most congested in Clackamas County during rush hour. Drivers transitioning from 65 mph highway speed to sudden stop-and-go at the exits cause rear-end crashes that result in serious whiplash at high differential speeds.

🌊
Hwy 43 (State St / Country Club Rd)

Hwy 43 runs along the Willamette through some of Oregon's most scenic — and most dangerous — commuting territory. The two-lane sections through West Linn and Lake Oswego have limited passing sight lines, and the road floods seasonally, creating year-round hazards. Head-on crashes on Hwy 43 are more common than other routes and produce severe injuries.

🏢
Kruse Way — Business District Crashes

Kruse Way through the Kruse Oaks corporate park sees heavy commuter traffic from some of Oregon's largest employers. The Kruse Way / Boones Ferry intersection and the SW Meadows Rd crossing are high-frequency rear-end and T-bone locations, particularly during morning rush hour when corporate park arrivals converge.

🏘️
Boones Ferry Rd — Residential to I-5 Connector

Boones Ferry Road is the primary connector between Lake Oswego residential neighborhoods and I-5. Its mix of residential driveways, school zones, and increasing traffic speeds creates the conditions for pedestrian conflicts and parking-exit crashes. The section near West Linn has seen a documented increase in accidents as development has intensified.
