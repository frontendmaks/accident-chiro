Hillsboro Accident Corridors

Where Hillsboro Accidents Happen — And Why

Hillsboro's mix of high-speed highways, light rail crossings, and high-density tech campuses creates a unique accident landscape in Washington County.
🛣️
US-26 (Sunset Highway) — Intel Corridor

The stretch of Hwy 26 through Hillsboro near Intel's Ronler Acres and Jones Farm campuses sees massive shift-change traffic twice daily. Merge conflicts and rear-end crashes on the ramps at NW 185th and Cornelius Pass are the most common patterns. High-speed rear-ends on the highway often cause far more serious injuries than they look.

🚃
TV Hwy (Tualatin Valley Highway) — MAX Crossings

TV Hwy through Hillsboro is one of Oregon's highest-accident arterials. MAX light rail crosses TV Hwy at multiple points, and the at-grade crossings create sudden stop situations that lead to rear-end pileups. Vehicles stopping suddenly for MAX gates cause the type of whiplash forces our specialists treat daily.

🏗️
Cornell Road — Construction Zone Crashes

Cornell Road connecting Hillsboro to Beaverton has seen ongoing development and lane reduction zones for years. Construction-zone crashes involve reduced sight lines and sudden speed changes — common setups for distracted-driver rear-ends. Washington County construction zone crash penalties are higher, which benefits injured parties.

🏢
NW Evergreen Pkwy — Business Park Exits

The business parks around NW Evergreen and Amberglen generate heavy traffic at predictable intervals tied to Intel and Nike shift changes. Parking lot exits and right-of-way confusion on the Pkwy lead to T-bone and sideswipe crashes, causing side-impact spinal injuries that are often underestimated by victims.
