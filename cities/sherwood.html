Sherwood Accident Corridors

Why Sherwood's Growth Is Creating More Accidents

Sherwood's population doubled between 2000 and 2020. The roads — particularly Hwy 99W and Sherwood Road — were not designed for current traffic volumes, creating dangerous conditions for commuters heading to the Portland metro every morning.

🛣️
Hwy 99W — Oregon's Dangerous Arterial

Hwy 99W through Sherwood carries over 35,000 vehicles per day on a road designed for far less. The combination of driveways, turn lanes, and high-speed through traffic creates constant rear-end and angle crash risks. The intersection at Sherwood Blvd and 99W is one of Washington County's most dangerous.

🌲
Tonquin Road — The Shortcut That Isn't Safe

Tonquin Road connects Sherwood to Tualatin through Tonquin Industrial Area. As a shortcut from 99W congestion, it's increasingly used by commuters — but its industrial-area speed limits, poor sight lines, and truck traffic create serious crash conditions, especially in low-light morning commutes.
