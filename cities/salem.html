I-5 Through Salem — Commercial Truck Hazard

I-5 through Salem carries the highest percentage of commercial truck traffic of any Oregon city. Agricultural produce trucks, timber haulers, and interstate freight create size and weight disparities in crashes that produce far more severe injuries than car-vs-car collisions. The I-5 / Mission St interchange sees the highest crash concentration.

🏛️
Commercial St SE — State Government Corridor

Commercial Street SE runs directly past the Oregon State Capitol and dozens of state agency buildings. Government shift changes twice daily send thousands of state workers into the street simultaneously, creating high-density intersection conflicts. The Commercial / Trade St intersection sees some of Salem's highest pedestrian-involved crash rates.

🌊
Hwy 22 Bridge Approaches — Marion County

The Hwy 22 bridge over the Willamette River creates merge conflicts where traffic transitions between the West Salem / Polk County side and Salem proper. Fog on the Willamette is a common seasonal hazard in October–March, contributing to multi-car rear-end pileups on the bridge approaches.

🌾
Lancaster Dr & South Salem Commercial

Lancaster Drive through South Salem is a high-commercial arterial where big-box retail and grocery traffic mixes with high-speed through commuters. Left-turn conflicts across multi-lane traffic and parking lot pull-outs create the classic South Salem accident pattern we see regularly in our clinic.
