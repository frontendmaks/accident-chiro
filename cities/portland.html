I-84 / I-205 Gateway Interchange

Portland's busiest interchange. Rear-end and merge-lane crashes happen daily during rush hour. Stop-and-go at 35–55 mph creates classic whiplash conditions — even minor-looking collisions cause serious cervical spine injuries.

82nd Ave & Powell Blvd Corridor

Portland's highest T-bone and angle crash rate. Side-impact crashes cause more serious spinal injuries than rear-ends — the cervical spine has no lateral protection. Disc herniations are common in 82nd Ave intersection crashes.

Burnside Bridge & Downtown Bridges

All five Willamette bridges funnel into narrow merge zones where fender-benders pile up daily. Parking garage exits on SW 3rd–6th Ave are especially common for low-speed, high-injury rear impacts that feel minor but cause lasting damage.

N Columbia Blvd — St. Johns Industrial

Heavy truck traffic on N Columbia creates mixed-traffic conditions where commercial vehicle crashes produce disproportionately severe injuries. Truck-versus-car collisions involve much higher force and more complex spinal damage patterns.
