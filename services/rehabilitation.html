Why Rehabilitation Is Just as Important as Adjustments

Spinal adjustments restore alignment and reduce pain. But the muscles, ligaments, and movement patterns that protect your spine can't be restored by manipulation alone. Without rehabilitation, many patients feel better temporarily — then reinjure the same area weeks or months later. Our rehab program addresses this directly.

Our Protocol

The 4-Phase Recovery Protocol

Each phase has specific goals, measurable outcomes, and criteria for progression. You don't advance until the tissue is genuinely ready — preventing the setbacks that occur when patients push too hard, too fast.

01

Acute Phase — Pain Control & Protection (Weeks 1–2)

Goals: Reduce inflammation, protect injured tissue from further damage, begin gentle movement to prevent scar tissue formation. Techniques: Ice/heat protocol, TENS, gentle range of motion exercises, breathing techniques to reduce pain-related muscle guarding. What we avoid: Any loading or strengthening — tissue needs to begin healing before it can be trained.

02

Subacute Phase — Stability Foundation (Weeks 2–6)

Goals: Rebuild deep stabilizer muscle activation, restore normal joint mechanics, begin proprioceptive retraining. Techniques: Transversus abdominis and deep cervical flexor activation, isometric exercises, gentle stability progressions, balance work. Key milestone: Patient can activate deep stabilizers on demand before progressing to Phase 3.

03

Strengthening Phase — Loading & Function (Weeks 6–12)

Goals: Progressive loading of injured structures, restore full range of motion, develop the strength to protect the spine under normal life loads. Techniques: Progressive resistance training, functional movement patterns (squat, hinge, push, pull), sport-specific or work-specific movements as appropriate. The strengthening phase is where most patients see the most dramatic recovery.

04

Return-to-Life Phase — Full Function (Weeks 10–16+)

Goals: Return to all pre-accident activities — work, exercise, sport, and daily life — without restriction. Techniques: High-demand functional movements, return-to-sport progressions where applicable, self-management education. Exit criteria: Full, pain-free range of motion, strength equal to or greater than pre-injury levels, patient is confident in their ability to manage their own spine health.
