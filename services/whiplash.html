The Science of Whiplash

What Actually Happens to Your Spine During a Crash

In a rear-end collision, your body accelerates forward in milliseconds while your head remains momentarily stationary — then snaps back. This "whip" motion extends the cervical spine (C1–C7) well beyond its normal range of motion, creating a cascade of damage:

Phase 1

Cervical hyperextension (0–100ms) — The lower cervical vertebrae retract and compress while the upper cervical spine extends. Facet joints become impacted and anterior muscles stretch beyond capacity.

Phase 2

Hyperflexion rebound (100–300ms) — The head then snaps forward. Posterior muscles, ligaments, and joint capsules are overstretched. Disc annular fibers may tear — an injury that won't show on X-ray but causes lasting instability.

Phase 3

Inflammatory cascade (hours to days) — Damaged tissue releases inflammatory cytokines. Swelling compresses nerves and restricts blood flow. This is why you feel worse on day 2 or 3 than you did immediately after the crash.

Why speed doesn't predict injury severity

Multiple studies confirm: vehicle damage correlates poorly with cervical spine injury. A 5 mph impact that barely dents a modern bumper can generate significant whiplash force if headrest height is wrong or the occupant is turned at impact. Never assume a "minor" crash means no injury.

Common Whiplash Symptoms

😣

Neck Pain & Stiffness

Typically worse on waking the day after — morning stiffness is a hallmark whiplash sign.

🤕

Sub-occipital Headaches

Starting at the base of the skull — caused by C1-C2 facet irritation and upper trapezius spasm.

😵

Dizziness & Vertigo

Cervicogenic dizziness from disrupted proprioceptive input in the upper cervical spine.

🖐️

Arm Numbness / Tingling

Nerve root irritation at C5–C7 — urgent sign of potential disc herniation requiring prompt evaluation.

🦾

Shoulder & Upper Back Pain

Referred pain from injured cervical facet joints often presents as shoulder and trapezius aching.

🧠

Cognitive Fog

Difficulty concentrating and memory issues — common with severe whiplash and any concurrent concussion.

Our Whiplash Protocol

How We Treat Whiplash — What You'll Actually Experience

Our whiplash protocol was designed specifically for motor vehicle accident injuries — not adapted from general chiropractic care. Every element targets the specific injury mechanisms of crash-induced cervical trauma.

Cervical adjustment — gentle, specific manipulation targeting the exact injured vertebral segments.

Myofascial release — reducing cervical muscle spasm and breaking down early scar tissue formation.

Cervical traction — decompressing disc spaces and reducing nerve root irritation from acute whiplash.

1

Comprehensive Cervical Assessment

Full range of motion testing, cervical compression and distraction tests (Spurling's, Jackson's), upper limb tension tests for nerve involvement, and proprioceptive screening. We create a detailed injury map — not just a description of where it hurts.

2

Spinal Manipulation — Targeted, Not Generic

Low-amplitude, specific adjustments to the restricted or malpositioned cervical and thoracic vertebrae. We avoid high-force manipulation in the acute phase — the protocol is adapted to your injury stage and tissue tolerance.

3

Soft Tissue Protocol — Scar Prevention

Instrument-assisted soft tissue mobilization (IASTM), trigger point therapy, and myofascial release targeting the sub-occipitals, sternocleidomastoid, scalenes, and upper trapezius — the muscles most damaged in whiplash. Early treatment prevents the scar tissue formation that makes whiplash chronic.

4

Deep Neck Stabilizer Rehabilitation

Whiplash damages the deep cervical flexors (longus colli and longus capitis) — the muscles responsible for cervical spine stability. Specific rehabilitation exercises rebuild these stabilizers, preventing the recurrence and chronification that happens when only pain is treated, not function.

5

Progress Documentation for Your Claim

Every visit is documented with objective measurements — range of motion in degrees, pain ratings, functional outcome scores. This creates the medical evidence trail your PI attorney needs. Our narrative reports are specifically formatted for Oregon personal injury claims.
