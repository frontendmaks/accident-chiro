What We Document

The 6 Records That Win Oregon Accident Claims

📋

Initial Injury Narrative

Created at your first visit — connects the crash mechanism to each specific injury using medical causation language that insurance reviewers and attorneys understand. Includes objective findings (ROM measurements, orthopedic test results) that establish injury severity from day one.

📈

Progress Notes (Every Visit)

Every treatment visit generates a SOAP-format progress note documenting your current status, treatment provided, and functional limitations. This visit-by-visit record creates an unbroken chain of medical evidence showing both your injuries and your ongoing impairment throughout recovery.

📊

Objective Outcome Measures

Numerical scores from validated outcome questionnaires (Neck Disability Index, Oswestry Low Back Disability Questionnaire) at intake and throughout treatment. These objective, standardized measures of functional impairment are difficult for insurance reviewers to dispute and quantify how your injuries have affected your daily life.

🔍

Causation Analysis

A written analysis establishing that your injuries were caused by or materially worsened by the crash — not pre-existing conditions. Insurance companies routinely claim injuries are "pre-existing" to avoid payment. Our causation documentation anticipates and rebuts this argument using the crash mechanism, timing of symptom onset, and your prior medical history.

📝

Final Narrative Report

At treatment conclusion, we produce a comprehensive narrative report summarizing your initial injury findings, complete treatment history, functional progress, and prognosis. This is the document your PI attorney presents in settlement negotiations or trial. Our narrative reports are formatted specifically for Oregon personal injury claim standards.

🛡️

IME Preparation Support

Insurance companies often require Independent Medical Examinations (IMEs) — which are rarely independent or favorable to patients. We prepare you for the IME process, ensure your records are complete before the examination, and provide a rebuttal report if the IME doctor's findings conflict with our documented clinical evidence.
