Our Approach

Soft Tissue Injuries — The Hidden Damage in Every Crash

🔬

Myofascial Release

Fascia is the connective tissue sheathing that surrounds every muscle in your body. In a crash, fascia tears and adheres to underlying muscle fibers, creating painful restrictions that limit movement and cause referred pain to distant areas. Our myofascial release technique uses sustained pressure to release these adhesions — restoring mobility and eliminating pain patterns that can't be resolved with stretching alone.

🎯

Trigger Point Therapy

Trigger points — hyperirritable nodules within taut muscle bands — form within hours of a crash injury. They cause characteristic referred pain patterns: a trigger point in the upper trapezius causes headache, one in the infraspinatus causes shoulder and arm pain, one in the gluteus minimus mimics sciatica. We map and deactivate every trigger point in the injury pattern, eliminating referred pain that would otherwise continue for months.

🔧

Instrument-Assisted Soft Tissue Mobilization (IASTM)

Using specialized stainless steel instruments, IASTM allows us to detect and treat fascial adhesions at depths unreachable by manual therapy. The instruments create controlled microtrauma that triggers the body's inflammatory-healing response, accelerating tissue remodeling and breaking down the fibrotic scar tissue that forms in the weeks after a crash injury.

🏃

Active Release Technique (ART)

ART is a patented movement-based technique specifically effective for overloaded and strained soft tissues. While applying precise tension to the affected tissue, we move the patient through specific motions — breaking the adhesions between muscle layers and restoring the smooth gliding motion between adjacent tissues that crash injuries disrupt.
