How Soon After a Car Accident Should You See a Chiropractor?

The answer is: as soon as possible — ideally within 72 hours. Here's why timing is critical for your health, your recovery, and your insurance claim.

After a car accident, many people delay seeking medical attention — especially if they don't feel immediate pain. This is one of the most common mistakes accident victims make, and it can have serious consequences for both their health and their legal claim.

The 72-Hour Window

Chiropractic care started within 72 hours of an accident consistently shows better outcomes than care started days or weeks later. Here's why this window matters:

  • Inflammation is still in its acute phase — easier to treat before it becomes chronic
  • Scar tissue hasn't yet formed — scar tissue restricts movement permanently if not addressed early
  • Muscle spasm is more responsive to treatment — easier to release in the first few days
  • Insurance documentation is stronger — records from day 1-3 clearly connect injuries to the accident

What Happens When You Wait Too Long

Each week you delay chiropractic care after an accident increases the risk of chronic injury. Within 2–3 weeks of untreated whiplash, compensatory patterns develop — your body begins moving differently to avoid pain, which creates new strain on surrounding structures.

Additionally, from an insurance perspective, adjusters are trained to argue that delayed treatment indicates the accident wasn't the cause of your injuries.

What if I Went to the ER First?

The ER is appropriate for serious acute injuries — head trauma, fractures, internal bleeding. However, ERs typically don't treat soft tissue injuries, whiplash, or spinal subluxation. After an ER visit, chiropractic care should begin as soon as possible — ideally within 24 hours of your discharge.

What if It's Been More Than 72 Hours?

Don't panic — later is still better than never. Even if it's been a week or two since your accident, chiropractic treatment will help your recovery. We will work with your specific situation and timeline. The key is to start now rather than continue waiting.
