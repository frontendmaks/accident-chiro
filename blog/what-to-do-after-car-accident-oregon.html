What to Do Immediately After a Car Accident in Oregon

The first 72 hours after a car accident in Oregon are critical — for your health, for your insurance claim, and for your legal rights. Here's exactly what to do.

Getting into a car accident is overwhelming. Adrenaline is pumping, your hands are shaking, and you're not sure what to do first. This guide walks you through exactly what to do — step by step — to protect your health and your legal rights in Oregon.

Step 1: Make Sure Everyone is Safe (First 5 Minutes)

Move your vehicle out of traffic if it's safe to do so. Turn on your hazard lights. Check yourself and your passengers for obvious injuries — but remember, adrenaline masks pain. You may feel fine even if you're seriously injured.

If anyone appears seriously injured, do not move them. Call 911 immediately.

Step 2: Call the Police

In Oregon, you are required by law to report accidents that result in injury, death, or property damage exceeding $2,500. Even in minor accidents, a police report creates an official record that protects you legally.

Wait for the officer and get the report number before you leave the scene.

Step 3: Exchange Information

Collect the following from all involved drivers:

  • Full name and contact information
  • Driver's license number
  • License plate number
  • Insurance company and policy number
  • Vehicle make, model, and year

Also collect contact information from any witnesses.

Step 4: Document Everything at the Scene

Use your phone to photograph: all vehicles and the damage, the road and intersection, skid marks, traffic signs, weather conditions, and any visible injuries. This evidence can be critical later.

✅ Important: Don't Admit Fault

Even saying "I'm sorry" can be used against you. Stick to factual statements only. Let the insurance companies and police determine fault based on evidence.

Step 5: Notify Your Insurance Company

Report the accident to your insurer promptly — Oregon law requires notification within a reasonable time. This is also how you activate your PIP (Personal Injury Protection) coverage, which will pay for your chiropractic care regardless of fault.

Step 6: See a Chiropractor Within 72 Hours

This is the most important step that most accident victims skip — and deeply regret later.

Even if you feel completely fine, your body has absorbed significant force. Whiplash, disc injuries, and soft tissue damage frequently have delayed symptoms — often appearing 24 to 72 hours after the accident when the adrenaline wears off.

⚠️ Why Waiting is Dangerous

Delaying chiropractic care has two serious consequences: (1) Your injuries may progress from acute to chronic, becoming much harder to treat. (2) Insurance adjusters use delay as evidence that you weren't seriously injured, weakening your claim.

What Happens at Your First Chiropractic Visit

A good auto accident chiropractor will conduct a comprehensive evaluation: full history of the accident and your symptoms, range of motion testing, orthopedic and neurological screening, and imaging if needed. You'll leave with a clear diagnosis and treatment plan.

And the critical thing to remember: this entire process costs you nothing. Oregon's PIP law requires your auto insurer to cover chiropractic care for accident injuries.

Oregon's 2-Year Statute of Limitations

Oregon gives you two years from the date of the accident to file a personal injury lawsuit. But don't let this create false comfort — the sooner you begin treatment, the stronger your claim and the better your recovery.
